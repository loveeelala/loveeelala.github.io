Reflection

The flexbox layout helps to support/constrain different userâ€™s experience by being able to neatly separate the title and the button. It ensures that users can quickly see where to click without making a mess. Requiring a click, or a keyboard control to reveal details gives users more control over how much information they consume at a time, making it easier to avoid distractions. However, it also means that users have to perform an extra action to get those details, which could make it more of a challenge for those looking to quickly and readily digest information.

With this micro-interaction, hiding information behind a toggle is making an assumption that summary text is always the most relevant starting point, which is not true for all users. Designers hold the power to decide what is immediately visible and what is hidden, and those choices influence who feels welcomed/excluded by the interface. While the toggle button can reduce visual clutter, it can also create barriers for some users, which is why in a larger Community Resource project, these design choices matter even more.
