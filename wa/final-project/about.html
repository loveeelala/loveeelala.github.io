I was born and raised in Hotchkiss, Colorado. Growing up in such a small town has really shaped the way I view community and what it means to me. At CU Boulder, I regularly put this perspective into practice as a peer mentor for the Lattice Program, where I am able to foster a close-knit community and support first-generation students in the program. I also worked as a mentor for the EngiNearMe program over the summer, where I was able to encourage high school students to explore STEM at the university.

When I began my journey at CU Boulder, I majored in Computer Science. Over time, I realized that the program felt too technical for me, so I made the decision to pursue Creative Technology & Design instead. To me, CTD is the best of both worlds. I’m still able to work with technology, but I also have the opportunity to express myself creatively and connect with others in what seem like a more human-centered environment.

Through these experiences, I’ve developed skills in C++ programming, 3D modeling (SketchUp and Blender), and design tools such as Photoshop. I enjoy using these skills and more to help bring my ideas to life. While my interests are currently broad, I’m excited to explore them further and narrow my focus as I progress in my degree.
