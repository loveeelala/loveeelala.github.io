Zoo Website Redesign

Redesign focusing on usability and visuals.

(PLACE HOLDER) Across all three user tests, participants expressed that the prototype lacked when it came to information throughout the experience. Users wanted clearer details about events on the homepage, including dates, summaries, and visuals. During the ticket-buying flow, participants were unsure what member versus non-member tickets included, what non-member tickets allowed access to, and how the time slots worked. Users also found the calendar to be unclear and suggested color-coding or adding more context. Another paint point was feeling like the flow had an unnecessary step.



My design updates will focus on incorporating more visuals to make the interface feel more cohesive and engaging. This includes adding supporting images on the homepage and throughout the ticket-buying process, as well as including a map of the zoo. My second design update will focus on adding more information into the pages. I plan to add event summaries, clearer explanations of what different ticket types include, and color-coding on the calendar. I also want to ensure that users don’t feel like they’re completing unnecessary steps.
