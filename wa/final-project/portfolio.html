My Projects

Zoo Website Redesign

A semester-long UX redesign of the Cheyenne Mountain Zoo’s “Buying a Ticket” flow, focused on improving clarity, reducing confusion, and creating a more user-friendly experience.

For this project I focused on reimagining the Cheyenne Mountain Zoo’s ticket-buying experience. Through usability testing, I found users felt the original flow felt very cluttered and at points, borderline difficult to navigate, especially for non-tech savvy families trying to plan a visit. I also worked on strengthening the visual hierarchy and designing a layout that feels more accessible. My Redesign aimed to simplify the process by creating a clearer distinction between ticket paths, improving the date and time selection screen, and improving the home screen layout.

Chess Set Model

I designed a portable chess set that could act as both the game board, and as storage for it.

For this project, I designed a woodlands-inspired chess set and created 3D models of the six game pieces. The original concept came with many challenges. I originally planned to build it out of wood by cutting and gluing each component, but the sliding-lid mechanism proved to be difficult to assemble. To solve for this, I redesigned the board using a laser-cutting approach, which allowed for me to standardize the entire board using a laser-cutting approach ensuring each piece fit together cleanly.



I modeled the six chess pieces in Blender and designed the board structure in SketchUp. This combination of 3D modeling helped me refine the fabrication of the final design.

Zine Cover

I designed a magazine cover themed around surveillance and panopticism. Using a blue and red color palette, surreal imagery, and typography I achieved this look.

I designed a magazine cover that aimed to explore themes of surveillance, privacy, and panopticism. I wanted the cover to feel unsettling, but clean by using a red and blue color palette. I made use of repeated eyes to represent the survelliance and a hand holding the "I" in the cover title. I think this use of negative space helped to create the tone I was looking for.
